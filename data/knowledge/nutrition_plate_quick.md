
# 营养配餐指南 | Nutrition Meal Planning Guidelines
用途：基于「运动营养学会（CSSN）」和「中国居民膳食指南2022」，提供减脂、增肌、日常健康三类场景的配餐方案，明确热量计算、宏量营养素比例、食物选择标准，帮助用户通过饮食辅助训练目标达成。

# 一、核心配餐原则（通用）
# 1. 热量计算（个性化基础）
- 每日总热量（TDEE）= 基础代谢率（BMR）× 活动系数
  - BMR计算公式（Mifflin-St Jeor公式）：
    - 男性：BMR = 10×体重（kg）+ 6.25×身高（cm）- 5×年龄 - 161
    - 女性：BMR = 10×体重（kg）+ 6.25×身高（cm）- 5×年龄 + 5
  - 活动系数：
    - 久坐（几乎不运动）：1.2
    - 轻度活动（每周1-3次训练）：1.375
    - 中度活动（每周3-5次训练）：1.55
    - 高度活动（每周6-7次训练）：1.725

- 目标调整：
  - 减脂：每日摄入 = TDEE - 300~500千卡（每周减重0.5-1kg，安全范围）
  - 增肌：每日摄入 = TDEE + 200~300千卡（每周增肌0.2-0.5kg，避免脂肪过多堆积）
  - 维持：每日摄入 = TDEE

# 2. 宏量营养素比例（按目标划分）
| 目标       | 蛋白质（g/kg体重） | 碳水化合物（占总热量） | 脂肪（占总热量） | 核心逻辑                                                                 |
|------------|--------------------|------------------------|------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| 减脂       | 1.8-2.2            | 40-45%                 | 25-30%           | 高蛋白提升饱腹感，减少肌肉流失；中低碳水控制血糖；健康脂肪维持激素水平     |
| 增肌       | 2.0-2.5            | 50-55%                 | 20-25%           | 高蛋白促进肌肉合成；高碳水提供训练能量；适量脂肪支持激素分泌（如睾酮）     |
| 日常健康   | 1.2-1.6            | 45-50%                 | 25-30%           | 均衡营养，兼顾代谢、免疫和身体机能                                       |

# 3. 食物选择优先级（按营养素分类）
# （1）蛋白质来源（吸收效率排序）
- 优质动物蛋白（吸收效率≥90%）：鸡蛋（1个=6g蛋白）、鸡胸肉（100g=22g）、三文鱼（100g=23g）、瘦牛肉（100g=20g）、低脂牛奶（100ml=3g）
- 优质植物蛋白（吸收效率60-80%）：豆腐（100g=8g）、鹰嘴豆（100g=7g）、藜麦（100g=4.4g）、黑豆（100g=8g）
- 禁忌：避免加工肉类（香肠、培根，含高盐、防腐剂）

# （2）碳水化合物来源（GI值排序）
- 低GI（GI<55，适合减脂/控糖）：燕麦（GI=42）、糙米（GI=55）、玉米（GI=55）、红薯（GI=54）、豆类（GI=30-40）
- 中GI（GI55-70，适合训练前后）：全麦面包（GI=69）、荞麦面（GI=60）、香蕉（GI=52-62，成熟度越高GI越高）
- 高GI（GI>70，仅训练后30分钟内使用）：葡萄糖、白米饭（GI=73）、白面包（GI=70）
- 禁忌：避免添加糖（奶茶、蛋糕、含糖饮料）

# （3）脂肪来源（健康优先级）
- 不饱和脂肪（推荐）：牛油果（100g=15g脂肪）、坚果（杏仁10颗=6g脂肪）、橄榄油（10ml=9g脂肪）、深海鱼（三文鱼100g=13g脂肪）
- 饱和脂肪（限量）：肥肉、黄油、奶酪（每日摄入量≤总热量的10%）
- 禁忌：反式脂肪（油炸食品、加工零食，如薯片、方便面）

# 4. 餐次分配原则
- 每日4-5餐（3正餐+1-2加餐），间隔3-4小时，避免长时间空腹（导致肌肉分解、血糖波动）
- 加餐选择：低热量、高营养密度（如1个苹果+10颗杏仁、1杯无糖酸奶+1小把蓝莓）
- 训练日特殊分配：
  - 训练前1-2小时：吃中GI碳水+少量蛋白质（如1片全麦面包+1个鸡蛋）
  - 训练后30分钟内：吃高GI碳水+优质蛋白质（如1根香蕉+1杯蛋白粉、1小碗白米饭+100g鸡胸肉）

# 三、特殊人群配餐调整
# 1. 素食者（无动物蛋白摄入）
- 蛋白质来源：豆腐、豆浆、鹰嘴豆、藜麦、坚果，需搭配食用（如米饭+豆类）提升氨基酸完整性
- 补充建议：每日补充维生素B12（1μg）、铁（15mg）、锌（10mg）（植物蛋白中这些营养素吸收效率低）
- 配餐示例：早餐（豆浆250ml+燕麦50g+核桃10g）、午餐（糙米饭100g+豆腐150g+西兰花200g）

# 2. 糖尿病患者（血糖控制优先）
- 碳水选择：仅低GI碳水，每餐碳水摄入量≤50g，避免单糖、双糖
- 餐次：每日5-6餐，少量多餐，避免血糖波动过大
- 禁忌：含糖饮料、精制碳水（白米饭、白面包）、油炸食品

# 3. 老年人（≥60岁）
- 蛋白质摄入量：1.6-1.8g/kg体重（肌肉流失风险高）
- 碳水选择：易消化的低GI碳水（如粥、烂面条、蒸红薯）
- 脂肪：增加不饱和脂肪摄入（如深海鱼、坚果），减少饱和脂肪
- 注意：餐量宜小，每日5-6餐，避免暴饮暴食

# 四、常见配餐误区纠正
# 1. 减脂误区
- 误区1：过度节食（每日摄入＜基础代谢）→ 导致肌肉流失、代谢下降、反弹
- 误区2：完全断碳水 → 影响训练表现、导致情绪波动（易怒、焦虑）、便秘
- 误区3：只吃水煮菜 → 营养不均衡（缺乏脂肪、维生素），难以坚持

# 2. 增肌误区
- 误区1：只吃蛋白粉 → 蛋白粉不能替代天然食物，过量摄入会增加肾脏负担
- 误区2：高热量=高营养 → 过多摄入油炸食品、甜食，导致脂肪堆积
- 误区3：训练后立即吃大量食物 → 训练后30分钟内只需补充20-30g蛋白+20-30g碳水，过量摄入会转化为脂肪

# 3. 日常健康误区
- 误区1：不吃脂肪 → 健康脂肪是激素合成的原料，缺乏会导致内分泌紊乱
- 误区2：主食=发胖 → 选择低GI碳水，控制分量，主食是身体主要能量来源
- 误区3：多吃水果代替蔬菜 → 水果含糖量高，蔬菜膳食纤维更丰富，不能替代

# 专业提示
1. 配餐需个性化：根据年龄、性别、训练目标、身体状况调整，不盲目照搬示例
2. 定期调整：每4周根据体重、体脂率、训练表现调整热量和宏量营养素比例
3. 饮水配合：每日饮水量=体重（kg）×35-40ml，训练日额外增加500-1000ml
4. 避免依赖补剂：营养优先通过天然食物获取，补剂仅作为补充（如蛋白粉、维生素）

# Nutrition Meal Planning Guidelines
Purpose: Based on the "Society for Sports Nutrition (CSSN)" and the "Chinese Dietary Guidelines 2022", it provides meal plans for three scenarios: fat loss, muscle building, and daily health. It clearly defines calorie calculation, macronutrient ratios, and food selection standards to help users achieve their training goals through dietary assistance.

# I. Core Meal Planning Principles (General)
# 1. Heat Calculation (Personalized Foundation)
Total daily energy expenditure (TDEE) = Basal metabolic rate (BMR) × activity coefficient
-BMR calculation formula (Mifflin-St Jeor formula) :
- Male: BMR = 10× weight (kg) + 6.25× height (cm) - 5× age - 161
For women: BMR = 10× weight (kg) + 6.25× height (cm) - 5× age + 5
- Activity coefficient:
- Prolonged sitting (almost no exercise) : 1.2
- Light activity (1-3 training sessions per week) : 1.375
Moderate activity (3-5 training sessions per week) : 1.55
- High activity (6-7 training sessions per week) : 1.725

- Goal Adjustment:
- Fat loss: Daily intake = TDEE - 300 to 500 kilocalories (0.5 to 1kg per week, safe range)
- Muscle building: Daily intake = TDEE + 200-300 kilocalories (Gain 0.2-0.5kg of muscle per week, avoid excessive fat accumulation)
- Maintenance: Daily intake = TDEE

# 2. Proportion of macronutrients (by target)
Objective: Protein (g/kg body weight); Carbohydrates (total calories); Fat (total calories); Core logic  |
|------------|--------------------|------------------------|------------------|----------------------------------------- ---------------------------------|
Fat loss: 1.8-2.2, 40-45%, 25-30%. High protein enhances satiety and reduces muscle loss. Control blood sugar with medium and low carb intake; Healthy fats maintain hormone levels
Muscle building: 2.0-2.5, 50-55%, 20-25%. High protein promotes muscle synthesis. High-carbohydrate provides training energy; Appropriate fat supports hormone secretion (such as testosterone)
Daily Health: 1.2-1.6-45-50% - 25-30% Balanced nutrition Balancing metabolism, immunity and physical functions

# 3. Food Choice Priority (Classified by Nutrients)
# (1) Protein sources (ranked by absorption efficiency)
- High-quality animal protein (absorption efficiency ≥90%) : egg (1 =6g of protein), chicken breast (100g=22g), salmon (100g=23g), lean beef (100g=20g), low-fat milk (100ml=3g)
- High-quality plant protein (absorption efficiency 60-80%) : Tofu (100g=8g), chickpeas (100g=7g), quinoa (100g=4.4g), black beans (100g=8g)
- Taboos: Avoid processed meats (sausages, bacon, high in salt and preservatives)

# (2) Sources of Carbohydrates (sorted by GI value)
- Low GI (GI<55, suitable for fat loss/sugar control) : Oats (GI=42), brown rice (GI=55), corn (GI=55), sweet potatoes (GI=54), beans (GI=30-40)
Medium GI (GI55-70, suitable before and after training) : Whole wheat bread (GI=69), buckwheat noodles (GI=60), bananas (GI=52-62, the higher the maturity, the higher the GI)
- High GI (GI>70, only used within 30 minutes after training) : glucose, white rice (GI=73), white bread (GI=70)
- Taboos: Avoid adding sugar (milk tea, cakes, sugary drinks)

# (3) Fat sources (Health Priority)
- Unsaturated fats (Recommended) : Avocado (100g=15g fat), nuts (10 almonds =6g fat), olive oil (10ml=9g fat), deep-sea fish (salmon 100g=13g fat)
- Saturated fats (limited) : Fatty meats, butter, cheese (daily intake ≤ 10% of total calories)
- Taboos: Trans fats (fried foods, processed snacks, such as potato chips, instant noodles)

# 4. Principles for Meal Allocation
Have 4 to 5 meals a day (3 main meals and 1 to 2 snacks), with an interval of 3 to 4 hours. Avoid prolonged fasting (which can cause muscle breakdown and blood sugar fluctuations).
- Snack options: Low calories, high nutrient density (such as 1 apple +10 almonds, 1 cup of sugar-free yogurt +1 small handful of blueberries)
Special allocation for training days:
- 1-2 hours before training: Consume medium-GI carbohydrates and a small amount of protein (such as 1 slice of whole wheat bread +1 egg)
Within 30 minutes after training: Consume high-GI carbohydrates and high-quality protein (such as 1 banana +1 cup of protein powder, 1 small bowl of white rice +100g of chicken breast)

# III. Adjustment of Meal Planning for Special Groups
# 1. Vegetarians (no intake of animal protein
- Protein sources: tofu, soy milk, chickpeas, quinoa, nuts. They should be consumed in combination (such as rice and beans) to enhance the integrity of amino acids
Supplementary suggestions: Take vitamin B12 (1μg), iron (15mg), and zinc (10mg) daily (these nutrients are not easily absorbed in plant protein).
Meal examples: Breakfast (250ml soy milk + 50g oats + 10g walnuts), lunch (100g brown rice + 150g tofu + 200g broccoli)

# 2. Diabetic patients (priority given to blood sugar control)
- Carbohydrate selection: Only use low-GI carbohydrates, with a carbohydrate intake of no more than 50g per meal. Avoid monosaccharides and disaccharides
- Meal frequency: 5 to 6 meals a day, small and frequent, to avoid excessive fluctuations in blood sugar
- Taboos: Sugary drinks, refined carbohydrates (white rice, white bread), fried foods

# 3. Elderly people (aged 60 and above)
- Protein intake: 1.6-1.8g/kg body weight (high risk of muscle loss)
- Carbohydrate selection: Easily digestible low-GI carbohydrates (such as porridge, soft noodles, steamed sweet potatoes)
- Fat: Increase the intake of unsaturated fats (such as deep-sea fish and nuts), and reduce saturated fats
Note: The meal size should be small, 5 to 6 meals a day, and avoid overeating

# Four. Correction of Common Meal Planning Misunderstandings
# 1. Misconceptions about Fat Loss
Misconception 1: Excessive dieting (daily intake < basal metabolic rate) → leads to muscle loss, decreased metabolism, and rebound
Misconception 2: Completely cutting off carbohydrates → It affects training performance, causes mood swings (irritability, anxiety), and constipation
Misconception 3: Only eating boiled vegetables → Unbalanced nutrition (lack of fat and vitamins), hard to stick to

# 2. Misconceptions about Muscle Building
Misconception 1: Only consuming protein powder → Protein powder cannot replace natural foods, and excessive intake will increase the burden on the kidneys
Misconception 2: High calories = high nutrition → Excessive intake of fried foods and sweets leads to fat accumulation
Misconception 3: Eating a large amount of food immediately after training → Only 20-30g of protein and 20-30g of carbohydrates need to be supplemented within 30 minutes after training. Excessive intake will be converted into fat

# 3. Daily Health Misconceptions
Misconception 1: Not eating fat → Healthy fat is the raw material for hormone synthesis. A deficiency can lead to endocrine disorders
Misconception 2: Staple foods = weight gain → Choose low-GI carbohydrates and control the portion size. Staple foods are the main source of energy for the body
Misconception 3: Eating more fruits instead of vegetables → Fruits have a high sugar content, while vegetables are richer in dietary fiber and cannot be replaced

# Professional Tips
1. Meal planning should be personalized: Adjust based on age, gender, training goals, and physical condition, and do not blindly copy examples
2. Regular adjustment: Every 4 weeks, adjust the proportion of calories and macronutrients based on body weight, body fat percentage, and training performance
3. Water intake coordination: Daily water intake = body weight (kg) ×35-40ml. On training days, an additional 500-1000ml should be consumed
4. Avoid relying on supplements: Prioritize obtaining nutrients from natural foods, and supplements should only be used as supplements (such as protein powder, vitamins).
