
# 恢复基础 | Recovery Fundamentals
用途：基于运动生理学和康复医学，提供科学的恢复原则，用于指导训练计划制定、避免过度训练，平衡训练效果与身体负荷。核心逻辑：恢复是训练效果的“催化剂”，无科学恢复则无持续进步。

# 一、核心恢复要素（量化标准+专业依据）
# 1. 睡眠恢复（训练后修复的核心环节）
# （1）基础标准
- 时长：成年人7-9小时/晚，青少年8-10小时/晚，老年人6-8小时/晚
- 睡眠结构：深度睡眠占比20-25%（促进肌肉修复、生长激素分泌），REM睡眠占比20-25%（恢复神经功能）
- 专业依据：生长激素在深度睡眠时分泌量是清醒时的3倍，而生长激素是肌肉修复、骨骼生长的关键激素（来源：运动生理学教材，人卫版）

# （2）睡眠质量优化方案
- 环境调整：卧室温度18-22℃（体温下降是入睡的信号），湿度50-60%，黑暗（使用遮光帘）、安静（噪音≤30分贝）
- 睡前禁忌：
  - 睡前3小时：不大量进食（尤其高蛋白、高脂肪食物）、不剧烈运动
  - 睡前1小时：不使用电子设备（蓝光抑制褪黑素分泌）、不摄入咖啡因（半衰期3-5小时）
- 辅助方法：睡前10分钟泡温水澡（体温先升后降，促进入睡）、喝1杯温牛奶（含色氨酸，助眠）

# （3）睡眠不足的应对
- 若睡眠≤6小时（成年人）：次日训练强度降低50%（如原RPE8改为RPE4-5），或改为低强度活动（如慢走）
- 若连续3天睡眠不足：暂停所有高强度训练，仅进行主动恢复（如泡沫轴放松、拉伸）

# 2. 低强度主动恢复（加速代谢废物排出）
# （1）执行标准
- 形式：慢走、瑜伽（阴瑜伽/修复瑜伽）、泡沫轴放松、太极基础动作、游泳（低强度）
- 强度：RPE3-4（轻松，能正常聊天，心率=（220-年龄）×40-50%）
- 时长：15-30分钟/次，训练后次日或减量周执行

# （2）专业依据
- 主动恢复可使血液循环速度提升20-30%，加速乳酸等代谢废物排出，缓解肌肉酸痛（DOMS）程度（来源：ACE运动恢复指南）
- 与被动休息（静坐/睡觉）相比，主动恢复可使肌肉酸痛持续时间缩短30%

# （3）人群适配
- 新手/恢复能力弱：选择慢走+泡沫轴放松（重点放松训练部位）
- 关节不适者：选择游泳（水的浮力可降低关节压力）
- 时间紧张者：10分钟泡沫轴放松（重点肌群：股四头肌、腘绳肌、背阔肌、斜方肌）

# 3. 饮水管理（维持内环境稳定）
# （1）基础饮水量
- 公式：每日基础饮水量（ml）= 体重（kg）×35-40ml（如60kg=2100-2400ml）
- 调整因素：
  - 训练日：每小时训练额外补充300-500ml（出汗多者可加电解质，如1L水+1g盐）
  - 高温环境（≥30℃）：额外增加500-1000ml/天
  - 疾病状态：感冒/发烧时，额外增加300-500ml/天

# （2）补水时机与方式
- 训练前：30分钟补充300-500ml温水
- 训练中：每15-20分钟补充100-200ml，避免一次性大量饮水（易引发肠胃不适）
- 训练后：30分钟内补充500-800ml，可搭配少量碳水（如香蕉），促进水分吸收

# （3）脱水判断标准
- 轻度脱水（体重下降1-2%）：口渴、尿液呈淡黄色，训练表现下降5-10%
- 中度脱水（体重下降3-5%）：头晕、乏力、尿液深黄色，需立即补水并暂停训练
- 专业提示：尿液颜色是最直观的判断依据（淡黄色=正常，深黄色=脱水，无色=饮水过多）

# 4. 蛋白质摄入（肌肉修复的原料）
# （1）摄入量标准（按人群划分）
| 人群类型               | 每日摄入量（g/kg体重） | 专业依据                          |
|------------------------|------------------------|-----------------------------------|
| 普通健康人群           | 1.0-1.2                | 维持肌肉量，满足基础代谢需求      |
| 力量训练新手           | 1.6-1.8                | 促进肌肉蛋白合成，加速恢复（NASM）|
| 进阶力量训练者         | 1.8-2.2                | 高强度训练后肌肉损伤修复需求增加  |
| 减脂期训练者           | 2.0-2.2                | 减少肌肉流失，提升饱腹感          |

# （2）食物来源与吸收效率
- 优质蛋白（吸收效率≥90%）：鸡蛋（1个=6g蛋白）、鸡胸肉（100g=22g蛋白）、三文鱼（100g=23g蛋白）、牛奶（100ml=3g蛋白）
- 植物蛋白（吸收效率60-80%）：豆腐（100g=8g蛋白）、鹰嘴豆（100g=7g蛋白）、藜麦（100g=4.4g蛋白）
- 专业提示：植物蛋白需搭配食用（如米饭+豆类），提升氨基酸完整性

# （3）分配原则
- 每餐摄入20-30g蛋白质（约1个鸡蛋+100g鸡胸肉），间隔3-4小时，避免一次性摄入过多（人体单次最多吸收30-40g蛋白）
- 训练后30分钟内补充蛋白质（搭配20-30g碳水），此时肌肉蛋白合成率最高（来源：运动营养杂志）

# 5. 训练间隔（避免过度训练）
# （1）肌群训练间隔
- 大肌群（腿、背、胸）：训练后需≥48小时恢复（肌肉蛋白合成高峰期为训练后24-72小时）
- 小肌群（肩、手臂、核心）：训练后需≥24小时恢复
- 专业依据：同一肌群短时间内重复高强度训练，会导致肌肉微损伤累积，增加受伤风险（来源：ACE力量训练指南）

# （2）训练强度间隔
- 高强度训练（RPE8-9）：两次之间需≥48小时恢复
- 中强度训练（RPE6-7）：两次之间需≥24小时恢复
- HIIT训练：每周最多1-2次，两次之间需≥72小时恢复（HIIT对心血管和神经消耗极大）

# 二、过度训练预警信号（需立即减量/休息）
# 1. 生理信号
- 静息心率：连续3天比平时高5-10次/分钟（如平时60次，现在65-70次）
- 运动表现：同样强度下，完成度下降20%以上（如原能做10次深蹲，现在只能做8次）
- 其他：持续疲劳、睡眠障碍（入睡困难/易醒）、免疫力下降（频繁感冒）、食欲减退

# 2. 心理信号
- 训练欲望显著下降（连续3天不想训练，甚至抵触）
- 情绪波动：易怒、焦虑、注意力不集中
- 专业提示：过度训练的核心是“训练负荷＞恢复能力”，此时需立即减少训练量50%，或休息1-2天

# 三、特殊人群恢复调整
# 1. 青少年（13-18岁）
- 睡眠：需8-10小时/晚（生长发育阶段，恢复需求更高）
- 训练间隔：大肌群间隔≥72小时，避免高强度训练
- 营养：蛋白质摄入量1.6-1.8g/kg体重，需额外补充钙（1000-1200mg/天）和维生素D（15μg/天）

# 2. 老年人（≥60岁）
- 训练间隔：大肌群间隔≥72小时，低强度训练为主
- 饮水：每日饮水量=体重（kg）×35ml，避免一次性大量饮水（肾脏功能下降）
- 恢复重点：关注关节疼痛，训练后多进行关节活动和拉伸

# 专业提示
1. 恢复计划需个性化：根据年龄、训练经验、身体状态调整，不盲目照搬他人方案
2. 定期评估恢复效果：每4周回顾训练日志，若出现过度训练信号，及时调整训练量
3. 避免“恢复误区”：
   - 误区1：训练后立即冰敷（仅适用于急性损伤，正常训练后冰敷会抑制肌肉修复）
   - 误区2：依赖补剂恢复（蛋白质补剂不能替代天然食物，基础营养优先通过饮食获取）

# Recovery Fundamentals
Usage: Based on sports physiology and rehabilitation medicine, it provides scientific recovery principles to guide the formulation of training plans, prevent overtraining, and balance training effects with physical load. The core logic: Recovery is the "catalyst" for training effectiveness. Without scientific recovery, there can be no continuous progress.

# I. Core Recovery Elements (Quantitative Standards + Professional Basis)
# 1. Sleep Recovery (The core link of post-training repair)
# (1) Basic Standards
Duration: 7-9 hours per night for adults, 8-10 hours per night for teenagers, and 6-8 hours per night for the elderly
- Sleep structure: Deep sleep accounts for 20-25% (promoting muscle repair and growth hormone secretion), and REM sleep accounts for 20-25% (restoring nerve function)
Professional basis: The secretion of growth hormone during deep sleep is three times that during wakefulness, and growth hormone is a key hormone for muscle repair and bone growth. (Source: Sports Physiology Textbook, People's Health Edition)

# (2) Sleep Quality Optimization Plan
- Environmental adjustment: Bedroom temperature: 18-22℃ (a drop in body temperature is a sign of falling asleep), humidity: 50-60%, dark (use blackout curtains), quiet (noise ≤30 decibels)
- Bedtime taboos:
- Three hours before going to bed: Do not eat a large amount of food (especially high-protein and high-fat foods), and do not engage in strenuous exercise
- One hour before going to bed: Do not use electronic devices (blue light inhibits melatonin secretion), and do not consume caffeine (with a half-life of 3-5 hours)
- Auxiliary methods: Take a warm bath 10 minutes before going to bed (body temperature rises first and then drops, promoting sleep), drink a cup of warm milk (containing tryptophan, aiding sleep)

# (3) Coping with Insufficient Sleep
- If sleep is no more than 6 hours (for adults) : Reduce the training intensity by 50% the next day (such as changing the original RPE8 to RPE4-5), or switch to low-intensity activities (such as slow walking).
If you lack sleep for three consecutive days: Suspend all high-intensity training and only engage in active recovery (such as foam axis relaxation and stretching).

# 2. Low-intensity active recovery (Accelerating the excretion of metabolic wastes)
# (1) Execution standards
- Forms: Slow walking, yoga (Yin Yoga/Repair Yoga), foam axis relaxation, basic Tai Chi movements, swimming (low intensity)
Intensity: RPE3-4 (Relaxed, able to chat normally, heart rate = (220- age) ×40-50%)
Duration: 15 to 30 minutes per session. Perform on the day after training or during the week of reduction

# (2) Professional basis
Active recovery can increase blood circulation by 20-30%, accelerate the excretion of metabolic wastes such as lactic acid, and alleviate the degree of DOMS (DOMS) muscle soreness (Source: ACE Exercise Recovery Guide)
Compared with passive rest (sitting still/sleeping), active recovery can shorten the duration of muscle soreness by 30%

# (3) Audience adaptation
- Beginners/Those with weak recovery ability: Choose slow walking + foam roller relaxation (focus on relaxing the training area)
For those with joint discomfort: Choose swimming (the buoyancy of water can reduce joint pressure).
- For those with tight schedules: 10 minutes of foam axis relaxation (key muscle groups: quadriceps, hamstrings, latissimus dorsi, trapezius)

# 3. Drinking Water Management (Maintaining Internal Environmental Stability)
# (1) Basic water intake
Formula: Daily basic water intake (ml) = Weight (kg) ×35-40ml (for example, 60kg=2100-2400ml)
- Adjusting factors:
- Training Day: Supplement an additional 300-500ml per hour of training (for those who sweat a lot, electrolytes can be added, such as 1L water +1g salt).
- High-temperature environment (≥30℃) : Add an additional 500-1000ml per day
- Disease status: When having a cold or fever, increase by an additional 300-500ml per day

# (2) Timing and Methods of Replenishing water
- Before training: Replenish 300-500ml of warm water 30 minutes later
During training: Replenish 100-200ml every 15-20 minutes. Avoid drinking large amounts of water at one time (as it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort).
- After training: Replenish 500-800ml within 30 minutes, which can be combined with a small amount of carbohydrates (such as bananas) to promote water absorption

# (3) Criteria for dehydration
Mild dehydration (weight loss of 1-2%) : Thirst, pale yellow urine, and a 5-10% decline in training performance
Moderate dehydration (weight loss of 3-5%) : Dizziness, fatigue, dark yellow urine. Immediate water replenishment and suspension of training are required
Professional tip: The color of urine is the most direct basis for judgment (light yellow = normal, dark yellow = dehydration, colorless = excessive water intake)

# 4. Protein Intake (Raw Material for Muscle Repair)
# (1) Intake Standards (by population)
Population type: Daily intake (g/kg body weight) : Professional basis
|------------------------|------------------------|-----------------------------------|
For the general healthy population: 1.0-1.2 Maintain muscle mass and meet basal metabolic requirements
Newcomers to strength Training: 1.6-1.8 Promote muscle protein synthesis and Accelerate recovery (NASM)
Advanced strength trainers: 1.8-2.2: Increased Demand for Muscle Injury Repair after High-intensity training
For fat loss trainers: 2.0-2.2: Reduce muscle loss and enhance satiety

# (2) Food Sources and Absorption Efficiency
- High-quality protein (absorption efficiency ≥90%) : egg (1 =6g protein), chicken breast (100g=22g protein), salmon (100g=23g protein), milk (100ml=3g protein)
- Plant protein (absorption efficiency 60-80%) : Tofu (100g=8g protein), chickpea (100g=7g protein), quinoa (100g=4.4g protein)
Professional tip: Plant protein should be consumed in combination (such as rice and beans) to enhance the integrity of amino acids

# (3) Distribution Principles
Consume 20-30g of protein per meal (about 1 egg +100g of chicken breast), with an interval of 3-4 hours. Avoid taking in too much at one time (the human body can absorb a maximum of 30-40g of protein in a single meal).
Within 30 minutes after training, supplement protein (combined with 20-30g of carbohydrates), at which point the muscle protein synthesis rate is the highest. (Source: Journal of Sports Nutrition)

# 5. Training Intervals (Avoid Overtraining)
# (1) Interval between muscle group training
- Large muscle groups (legs, back, chest) : Recovery should take at least 48 hours after training (the peak period for muscle protein synthesis is 24 to 72 hours after training).
- Small muscle groups (shoulders, arms, core) : It takes at least 24 hours to recover after training
Professional basis: Repeating high-intensity training for the same muscle group within a short period of time can lead to the accumulation of micro-muscle injuries and increase the risk of injury. (Source: ACE Strength Training Guide)

# (2) Training intensity intervals
- High-intensity training (RPE8-9) : At least 48 hours of recovery is required between two sessions
Moderate-intensity training (RPE6-7) : At least 24 hours of recovery is required between sessions
- HIIT training: No more than 1-2 times per week, with at least 72 hours of recovery between sessions (HIIT consumes a great deal of energy on the cardiovascular and nervous systems).

# II. Warning Signs of Overtraining (Immediate Reduction of Training Volume/Rest Required)
# 1. Physiological signals
- Resting heart rate: 5 to 10 beats per minute higher than usual for three consecutive days (for example, 60 beats per minute usually, 65 to 70 beats per minute now)
- Athletic performance: Under the same intensity, the completion rate drops by more than 20% (for example, if one could originally do 10 squats, they can now only do 8).
- Others: Persistent fatigue, sleep disorders (difficulty falling asleep/easy awakening), weakened immunity (frequent colds), loss of appetite

# 2. Psychological Signals
- A significant decrease in the desire to train (not wanting to train for three consecutive days, even resisting)
- Mood swings: Irritability, anxiety, and difficulty concentrating
Professional tip: The core of overtraining is "training load > recovery capacity". At this point, it is necessary to immediately reduce the training volume by 50% or rest for 1-2 days

# III. Recovery and Adjustment for Special Groups
# 1. Teenagers (aged 13-18)
- Sleep: 8-10 hours per night (During the growth and development stage, the recovery requirement is higher)
- Training interval: For large muscle groups, the interval should be no less than 72 hours. Avoid high-intensity training
- Nutrition: Protein intake is 1.6-1.8g per kg of body weight. Additional calcium (1000-1200mg per day) and vitamin D (15μg per day) should be supplemented.

# 2. Elderly people (aged 60 and above)
- Training interval: For large muscle groups, the interval should be no less than 72 hours, with low-intensity training as the main focus
- Water intake: Daily water intake = weight (kg) ×35ml. Avoid drinking large amounts of water at one time (as kidney function declines).
Recovery focus: Pay attention to joint pain and do more joint movement and stretching after training

# Professional Tips
The recovery plan should be personalized: adjusted based on age, training experience and physical condition, and not blindly copy others' plans
2. Regularly evaluate the recovery effect: Review the training log every four weeks. If there are signs of overtraining, adjust the training volume in a timely manner
3. Avoid "recovery pitfalls" :
Misconception 1: Applying ice immediately after training (only applicable to acute injuries; applying ice after normal training will inhibit muscle repair)
Misconception 2: Relying on supplements for recovery (Protein supplements cannot replace natural foods, and basic nutrients should be obtained through diet first)
