
# 热身与放松指南 | Warmup & Cooldown Guidelines
用途：基于运动生理学，提供科学的热身和放松流程，减少运动损伤风险（热身可降低受伤风险30-40%），提升训练表现（热身可使训练表现提升10-15%），加速恢复。

# 一、热身流程（训练前10-12分钟，分3个阶段）
# 阶段1：轻度有氧激活（3-5分钟）
# 核心目标：提升心率（至最大心率的50-60%）、升高体温（肌肉温度每升高1℃，力量提升2-3%）、促进血液循环
# 推荐动作（选1个）
- 跑步机慢走/慢跑：速度4-6km/h，保持能正常聊天
- 椭圆机：中等阻力，双脚交替踩踏，保持上肢稳定
- 动态自行车：阻力1-2档，每分钟踩踏60-80次
- 无器械替代：原地高抬腿（慢速度）、开合跳（轻幅度），各1分钟×2组

# 专业依据：肌肉温度升高可降低肌肉粘滞性，减少肌肉拉伤风险；心率提升可使心血管系统提前适应训练负荷（来源：ACE运动损伤预防指南）

# 阶段2：关节活动度训练（2-3分钟）
# 核心目标：活动全身关节，提升关节灵活性，避免训练中关节卡压
# 动作组合（每个关节各10次，顺时针+逆时针各5次）
- 颈部：缓慢左右转头、上下点头、环绕（幅度适中，避免用力过猛）
- 肩部：手臂环绕（向前、向后各10次）、肩胛骨收缩/扩张（10次）
- 胸椎：转体（坐姿/站姿，双手交叉放胸前，左右转体各10次）
- 髋关节：髋关节环绕（双手扶髋，左右腿交替环绕各10次）
- 膝关节：缓慢弯曲/伸直（10次，避免锁膝）
- 踝关节：勾脚/绷脚（各10次）、环绕（各10次）

# 注意事项：关节活动时保持缓慢平稳，避免弹响和疼痛，幅度逐步增加（从小到大）

# 阶段3：动态拉伸（4-5分钟）
# 核心目标：激活肌肉群、提升肌肉弹性、强化神经肌肉控制，为训练动作做准备（动态拉伸≠静态拉伸，动态拉伸是主动收缩肌肉，静态拉伸是被动放松）
# 推荐动作（每个动作10-12次/组，做1组，针对训练日重点肌群）
| 动作名称         | 针对肌群       | 技术要点                                                                 |
|------------------|----------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| 徒手深蹲         | 股四头肌、臀大肌 | 双脚与肩同宽，脚尖微外展，下蹲至大腿与地面平行，快速站起，保持核心收紧     |
| 弓步走           | 股四头肌、腘绳肌 | 向前迈一步，下蹲至前后腿均呈90°，快速站起并换腿，避免前膝超脚尖           |
| 手臂环绕         | 三角肌、肩袖肌群 | 双手握轻量哑铃（1-2kg），向前、向后环绕各10次，幅度逐步增加               |
| 俯身摆腿         | 腘绳肌、髋关节  | 上半身前倾45°，双腿交替前后摆动（幅度适中），保持背部挺直                 |
| 侧弓步           | 内收肌、股四头肌 | 双脚分开大于肩宽，向一侧下蹲，另一侧腿伸直，左右交替各10次                |
| 高抬腿（原地）   | 髂腰肌、股四头肌 | 膝盖抬高至胸前，快速交替，保持上半身稳定，避免晃动                       |

# 专业依据：动态拉伸可激活肌肉中的本体感受器（肌梭），提升神经肌肉传导速度，使训练时动作更协调（来源：NASM热身指南）
# 常见错误：动态拉伸时速度过快、幅度过大，导致肌肉代偿，应保持动作可控、幅度适中

# 二、放松流程（训练后10-12分钟，分3个阶段）
# 阶段1：减速有氧（3-4分钟）
# 核心目标：逐步降低心率（从训练时的80%最大心率降至60%以下）、促进乳酸代谢（避免乳酸堆积导致的肌肉酸痛）
# 推荐动作（选1个）
- 跑步机慢走：速度3-4km/h，持续3-4分钟
- 椭圆机：低阻力，缓慢踩踏，持续3-4分钟
- 无器械替代：原地缓慢踏步+深呼吸，持续3-4分钟

# 注意事项：避免训练后立即停止活动（如直接坐下/躺下），会导致血液淤积在下肢，引发头晕、乏力

# 阶段2：静态拉伸（5-6分钟）
# 核心目标：放松肌肉、降低肌肉张力、改善柔韧性、缓解延迟性肌肉酸痛（DOMS）
# 推荐动作（每个动作保持30-45秒，做1组，针对训练日重点肌群）
| 动作名称         | 针对肌群       | 技术要点                                                                 |
|------------------|----------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| 大腿前侧拉伸     | 股四头肌       | 站姿，单手扶墙，另一只手抓住脚踝拉向臀部，保持膝盖并拢，身体直立，避免弯腰 |
| 大腿后侧拉伸     | 腘绳肌         | 坐姿，一腿伸直，一腿弯曲，上半身前倾至感受大腿后侧拉伸，保持背部挺直       |
| 臀部拉伸         | 臀大肌         | 仰卧，一腿弯曲，脚踝放在另一腿膝盖上，双手抱住下方大腿拉向胸口，左右交替   |
| 胸部拉伸         | 胸大肌、三角肌前束 | 站姿，双手抓住门框/墙壁，手臂与肩同高，身体向前倾，感受胸部拉伸           |
| 背部拉伸         | 背阔肌、斜方肌 | 坐姿，双腿伸直，双手向前伸展，上半身前倾，感受背部拉伸                   |
| 肩颈拉伸         | 斜方肌上束     | 坐姿，一手扶头侧部，轻轻向对侧拉动，另一手自然下垂，保持肩膀下沉           |
| 小腿拉伸         | 腓肠肌、比目鱼肌 | 站姿，双手扶墙，一腿在前弯曲，一腿在后伸直，脚后跟贴地，身体向前倾         |

# 专业依据：静态拉伸可降低肌肉紧张度，增加肌肉柔韧性，训练后拉伸可使肌肉酸痛持续时间缩短20-30%（来源：ACE恢复指南）
# 执行原则：
- 拉伸时感受“轻微酸胀”即可，避免疼痛（疼痛会导致肌肉紧张，反而达不到放松效果）
- 保持呼吸均匀，不憋气（憋气会增加肌肉张力）
- 每个动作保持30-45秒（肌肉纤维完全放松需要至少30秒）

# 阶段3：泡沫轴放松（2-3分钟，可选）
# 核心目标：深层放松肌肉筋膜、缓解肌肉结节（扳机点）、改善肌肉血液循环
# 推荐动作（每个部位滚动30秒，针对训练后酸痛明显的肌群）
- 股四头肌：俯卧，泡沫轴放在大腿下方，缓慢滚动（重点滚动酸痛部位）
- 腘绳肌：坐姿，泡沫轴放在大腿后侧，双手支撑地面，缓慢滚动
- 背阔肌：侧卧，泡沫轴放在背部侧面，缓慢滚动
- 斜方肌：坐姿，泡沫轴放在背部上方，双手抱头，缓慢滚动

# 注意事项：
- 滚动速度缓慢（每秒1-2厘米），遇到酸痛结节时，停留10-15秒，深呼吸放松
- 避免在关节、脊柱、骨骼突出部位滚动（如膝盖、腰椎、肩胛骨）
- 新手使用时选择软质泡沫轴，避免硬质泡沫轴（易导致肌肉损伤）

# 三、特殊场景调整
# 1. 短时间训练（＜30分钟）
- 热身：简化为5分钟（轻度有氧2分钟+动态拉伸3分钟）
- 放松：简化为5分钟（静态拉伸5分钟）

# 2. 高温环境（≥30℃）
- 热身：轻度有氧时间缩短至2分钟（避免过度出汗），重点增加关节活动度训练
- 放松：增加减速有氧时间至5分钟，补充水分和电解质

# 3. 低温环境（≤10℃）
- 热身：轻度有氧时间延长至5分钟（需更多时间升高体温），动态拉伸幅度逐步增加
- 放松：缩短户外放松时间，尽量在室内进行，避免着凉

# 四、常见误区纠正
# 1. 热身误区
- 误区1：用静态拉伸代替动态拉伸（训练前静态拉伸会降低肌肉力量和爆发力，影响训练表现）
- 误区2：热身时间过短（＜5分钟），肌肉温度和心率未达到训练要求
- 误区3：热身动作与训练动作无关（如训练下肢却重点热身上肢）

# 2. 放松误区
- 误区1：训练后立即冰敷（仅适用于急性损伤，正常训练后冰敷会抑制肌肉修复）
- 误区2：拉伸时用力过猛（导致肌肉拉伤）
- 误区3：忽略小肌群放松（如肩袖肌群、内收肌）

# 专业提示
1. 热身和放松是训练的必要环节，不可省略（尤其是新手和高强度训练）
2. 个性化调整：根据训练内容调整热身和放松重点（如训练下肢，重点热身/放松下肢肌群）
3. 反馈调整：若训练中出现关节不适，可能是热身不充分，下次需增加对应关节的活动度训练

# Warmup & Cooldown Guidelines
Usage: Based on sports physiology, it provides a scientific warm-up and relaxation process to reduce the risk of sports injuries (warm-up can lower the risk of injury by 30-40%), enhance training performance (warm-up can improve training performance by 10-15%), and accelerate recovery.

# I. Warm-up Process (10-12 minutes before training, divided into 3 stages)
# Stage 1: Mild Aerobic Activation (3-5 minutes)
# Core Objectives: Increase heart rate (to 50-60% of maximum heart rate), raise body temperature (for every 1℃ increase in muscle temperature, strength increases by 2-3%), and promote blood circulation
# Recommended Action (Choose 1)
- Treadmill slow walking/jogging: Speed 4-6km/h, maintaining normal chatting ability
- Elliptical machine: Moderate resistance. Alternate pedaling with both feet to keep the upper limbs stable
Dynamic bicycle: Resistance 1-2 gears, pedaling 60-80 times per minute
- No equipment substitution: High knee lifts in place (slow speed), jumping jacks (light range), 1 minute each ×2 sets

# Professional basis: An increase in muscle temperature can reduce muscle viscosity and lower the risk of muscle strain. An increase in heart rate can enable the cardiovascular system to adapt to training loads earlier. (Source: ACE Sports Injury Prevention Guidelines)

Phase 2: Joint range of Motion Training (2-3 minutes)
# Core Objective: Move all joints throughout the body, enhance joint flexibility, and prevent joint compression during training
# Movement Combinations (10 repetitions for each joint, 5 clockwise and 5 counterclockwise)
- Neck: Slowly turn your head left and right, nod up and down, and wrap around (with moderate movements to avoid excessive force).
- Shoulders: Arm encirclement (10 times forward and 10 times backward), scapula contraction/expansion (10 times)
- Thoracic vertebrae: Rotation (sitting/standing position, cross hands in front of the chest, rotate left and right 10 times each)
- Hip joint: Hip joint encirclement (Place both hands on the hips and alternate between the left and right legs for 10 times each)
- Knee joint: Slowly bend/straighten (10 times, avoid locking the knee)
- Ankle joint: Hook/stretch (10 times each), wrap (10 times each)

# Precautions: When moving the joints, keep them slow and steady to avoid clicking and pain. Gradually increase the range of motion (from small to large).

# Stage 3: Dynamic Stretching (4-5 minutes)
# Core Objective: Activate muscle groups, enhance muscle elasticity, strengthen neuromuscular control, and prepare for training movements (dynamic stretching ≠ static stretching; dynamic stretching is active muscle contraction, while static stretching is passive relaxation)
# Recommended Movements (10-12 repetitions per set for each movement, do 1 set, targeting the key muscle groups on the training day)
"Action Name: Target muscle Group: Technical Key Points
|------------------|----------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|
Squats without equipment: Quadriceps, gluteus maximus. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, toes slightly outwards, and squat until your thighs are parallel to the ground. Stand up quickly and keep your core tightened
Lunge walk: Quadriceps, hamstrings: Take a step forward, squat until both the front and back legs are at a 90° Angle, stand up quickly and switch legs to avoid the front knee going beyond the toes
Arm rotation: Deltoid muscles, rotator cuff muscles. Hold light dumbbells (1-2kg) with both hands and rotate forward and backward 10 times each, gradually increasing the range of motion
Bend over and swing your legs. Hamstrings and hip joints. Lean your upper body forward at a 45-degree Angle and swing your legs alternately back and forth (with moderate amplitude) to keep your back straight
Side lunges: Adductor muscles, quadriceps. Stand with your feet wider than your shoulders, squat to one side while keeping the other leg straight. Alternate between the left and right sides 10 times each
High leg raises (in place) : Iliopsoas and quadriceps muscles: Raise your knees to your chest, alternate quickly, keep your upper body stable and avoid shaking

# Professional Basis: Dynamic stretching can activate the proprioceptive receptors (muscle spasms) in the muscles, increase the speed of neuromuscular conduction, and make the movements during training more coordinated (Source: NASM Warm-up Guide)
# Common Mistakes: When doing dynamic stretching, if the speed is too fast or the amplitude is too large, it may cause muscle compensation. One should keep the movement controllable and the amplitude moderate

# II. Relaxation Process (10-12 minutes after training, divided into 3 stages)
# Stage 1: Decelerated Aerobic Exercise (3-4 minutes)
# Core Objective: Gradually reduce heart rate (from 80% of the maximum heart rate during training to below 60%) and promote lactic acid metabolism (to avoid muscle soreness caused by lactic acid accumulation)
# Recommended Action (Choose 1)
- Treadmill slow walk: Speed 3-4km/h, for 3-4 minutes
- Elliptical machine: Low resistance, pedal slowly for 3 to 4 minutes
- No equipment replacement: Slowly step in place and take deep breaths for 3 to 4 minutes

# Precautions: Avoid stopping activities immediately after training (such as sitting down or lying down directly), as this can cause blood to accumulate in the lower limbs, leading to dizziness and fatigue

# Stage 2: Static Stretching (5-6 minutes)
# Core Objectives: Relax muscles, reduce muscle tone, improve flexibility, and relieve delayed onset muscle soreness (DOMS)
# Recommended Movements (Hold each movement for 30-45 seconds, do one set, targeting the key muscle groups for the training day)
"Action Name: Target muscle Group: Technical Key Points
|------------------|----------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|
Thigh front stretch: Quadriceps: Stand with one hand against the wall and the other hand grasping the ankle and pulling towards the hip. Keep the knees together and stand upright, avoiding bending over
Back thigh stretch: Hamstrings: Sit with one leg straight and the other bent, lean your upper body forward to feel the stretch on the back of your thighs, and keep your back straight
Gluteus maximus stretch: Lie on your back, bend one leg, place your ankle on the knee of the other leg, hold the lower thigh with both hands and pull it towards your chest, alternating left and right
Chest stretch: Pectoralis major, anterior deltoid muscle. Stand with both hands grasping the door frame or wall, arms at shoulder height, lean forward, and feel the chest stretch
Back Stretch: latissimus dorsi, trapezius muscles. Sit with your legs straight, hands stretched forward, and your upper body leaning forward. Feel the back stretch
Shoulder and neck stretch: Upper trapezius muscle. Sit with one hand on the side of your head and gently pull it to the opposite side. Let the other hand hang naturally to keep your shoulders sinking
Calf stretch: Gastrocnemius muscle, soleus muscle. Stand with both hands against the wall. Bend one leg in front and extend the other straight behind. Keep the heels on the ground and lean forward

# Professional Basis: Static stretching can reduce muscle tension and increase muscle flexibility. Stretching after training can shorten the duration of muscle soreness by 20-30% (Source: ACE Recovery Guide)
# Execution Principle:
When stretching, just feel a slight soreness and distension. Avoid pain (pain can cause muscle tension and instead fail to achieve the relaxing effect).
Keep your breathing even and don't hold your breath (holding your breath will increase muscle tension).
Hold each movement for 30 to 45 seconds (at least 30 seconds are needed for the muscle fibers to fully relax).

# Stage 3: Foam Axis Relaxation (2-3 minutes, optional)
# Core Objective: Deeply relax the muscle fascia, relieve muscle nodules (trigger points), and improve muscle blood circulation
# Recommended Movement (Roll each part for 30 seconds, targeting muscle groups that experience significant soreness after training)
- Quadriceps: Lie prone, place the foam roller under the thigh, and slowly roll (focusing on the sore area).
- Hamstrings: Sit with a foam roller at the back of your thighs, support yourself on the ground with both hands, and roll slowly
- latissimus dorsi: Lie on your side, place the foam roller on the side of your back, and roll slowly
- Trapezius muscle: Sit with a foam roller placed above your back, hold your head with both hands, and roll slowly

# Notes
Roll slowly (1-2 centimeters per second). When encountering sore nodules, hold for 10-15 seconds and take deep breaths to relax
Avoid rolling on protruding parts of the joints, spine and bones (such as the knees, lumbar vertebrae and shoulder blades).
Novices should choose soft foam shafts when using them and avoid hard foam shafts (which can easily cause muscle damage).

# III. Special Scene Adjustments
# 1. Short-term training (< 30 minutes)
- Warm-up: Simplified to 5 minutes (2 minutes of light aerobic exercise + 3 minutes of dynamic stretching)
- Relaxation: Simplified to 5 minutes (static stretching for 5 minutes)

# 2. High-temperature environment (≥30℃
- Warm-up: Reduce the duration of light aerobic exercise to 2 minutes (avoid excessive sweating), and focus on increasing joint range of motion training
- Relaxation: Increase the deceleration aerobic time to 5 minutes to replenish water and electrolytes

# 3. Low-temperature environment (≤10℃
- Warm-up: Extend the duration of light aerobic exercise to 5 minutes (more time is needed to raise body temperature), and gradually increase the range of dynamic stretching
- Relaxation: Reduce the time spent relaxing outdoors and try to do it indoors as much as possible to avoid catching a cold

# IV. Correction of Common Misunderstandings
# 1. Warm-up Misconceptions
Misconception 1: Replacing dynamic stretching with static stretching (Static stretching before training will reduce muscle strength and explosive power, affecting training performance)
Misconception 2: The warm-up time is too short (less than 5 minutes), and the muscle temperature and heart rate do not meet the training requirements
Misconception 3: Warm-up exercises have nothing to do with training exercises (for example, training the lower limbs but focusing on warming up the upper limbs)

# 2. Relax Misunderstandings
Misconception 1: Applying ice immediately after training (only applicable to acute injuries; applying ice after normal training will inhibit muscle repair)
Misconception 2: Excessive force during stretching (resulting in muscle strain)
Misconception 3: Neglecting the relaxation of small muscle groups (such as the rotator cuff and adductor muscles)

# Professional Tips
Warming up and relaxation are essential parts of training and cannot be omitted (especially for beginners and those undergoing high-intensity training).
2. Personalized adjustment: Adjust the focus of warm-up and relaxation based on the training content (for example, when training the lower limbs, focus on warming up/relaxing the lower limb muscle groups)
3. Feedback Adjustment: If joint discomfort occurs during training, it might be due to insufficient warm-up. Next time, you need to increase the range of motion training for the corresponding joints
